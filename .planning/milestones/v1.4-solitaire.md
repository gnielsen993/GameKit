# Milestone Sketch: v1.4 — Solitaire

**Status:** Idea — not committed  
**Type:** Minor (new game milestone, two games)  
**Created:** 2026-05-22

---

## What ships

Two card games: Klondike Solitaire and FreeCell. Klondike is the familiar drag-to-stack game. FreeCell is the personal priority — all 52 cards dealt face-up at the start, four free cells as temporary holds, four foundation piles to build by suit. Almost every deal is winnable with correct play, making it a pure logic game with no luck element after the deal.

---

## Game 1: FreeCell (priority)

**Why priority:** Pure logic, zero luck (all cards visible from deal), fits the GameDrawer "logic game" brand better than Klondike.

### Mechanics
- 52 cards dealt across 8 columns, all face-up
- 4 free cells: each holds exactly one card temporarily
- 4 foundation piles: build A → K by suit
- Move rules: a card can move onto a column card if it is one rank lower and opposite color; a sequence of N cards can move in one gesture if enough free cells + empty columns exist to support it (the "supermove" rule — implement this; it's the standard FreeCell behavior)
- Win: all 52 cards on foundations
- Loss: no legal moves remain (rare — only ~0.1% of deals are unsolvable; standard practice is to exclude known-unsolvable deals)

### Deal numbering
- Traditional FreeCell uses a specific deal-numbering system (Microsoft's 1–1,000,000 range). Support deal numbers so users can share a specific deal ("play deal #617").
- Ship with deal #1–32,000 (the classic range); exclude the ~8 known unsolvable deals.
- Random deal option in addition to numbered deals.

---

## Game 2: Klondike Solitaire

### Mechanics
- Standard 7-column tableau, 24-card stock with draw-1 or draw-3 option (setting per game, not global)
- Tap stock to draw; tap or drag to move cards
- Auto-complete when all cards are face-up and sorted (optional, gated by setting)
- Win detection: all foundations built to K

## Card design — the core challenge

Cards must work across all 17 DesignKit presets. This rules out photorealistic or highly styled illustrated cards. The approach that works within DesignKit's token system:

### Card anatomy
- **Card surface:** `theme.colors.cardSurface` (white in Classic, dark in Dracula, etc.) — this token already exists; cards are a perfect use case
- **Card border/shadow:** subtle, uses `theme.radii.card`
- **Rank + suit (corner):** top-left and bottom-right — standard layout
- **Center suit symbol:** large, for face recognition at a glance
- **Face cards (J/Q/K):** Styled letter + suit — no illustrated royals needed. A large "Q" with a suit symbol below it reads clearly and looks intentional, not cheap. This sidesteps the entire illustration problem.
- **Suit colors:** Red suits (♥ ♦) use `theme.colors.danger`; Black suits (♠ ♣) use `theme.colors.label` (primary text color). These two tokens are guaranteed to be legible and contrast against the card surface on every preset.

### Suit symbols
Use SF Symbols: `suit.heart.fill`, `suit.diamond.fill`, `suit.club.fill`, `suit.spade.fill` — these exist in SF Symbols 4+ and render cleanly at all sizes.

### Classic preset special treatment
Classic (Chrome Diner) is the one preset where cards can have a more traditional character:
- Card surface: white (already the default)
- Felt board: `theme.colors.gameBoard` anchor override — green felt per CLAUDE.md §1 Classic restomod policy. This is the first game that actually needs the `@Environment(\.classicAnchorOverride)` hook described in CLAUDE.md. Wire it here.
- Face card styling: consider a thin decorative border inside the card frame (a ruled inset, 1pt line) — purely cosmetic, reads as "traditional card" without serif fonts or skeuomorphic depth. One line of SwiftUI code.
- All other presets: plain card frame, no inset border.

### Stock / waste pile presentation
- Stock: face-down card stack (just the back — a solid color block with a subtle pattern using `theme.colors.accent` at low opacity, or a simple grid/diamond pattern in SwiftUI)
- Waste: face-up, fan-stacked showing top 1 or top 3 depending on draw mode

## Board layout

### FreeCell
```
[ FC ] [ FC ] [ FC ] [ FC ]   [ F♠ ] [ F♥ ] [ F♦ ] [ F♣ ]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[ col ] [ col ] [ col ] [ col ] [ col ] [ col ] [ col ] [ col ]
```
- Top row: 4 free cells (left) + 4 foundations (right)
- 8 columns fill the remaining height with `layoutPriority(1)`

### Klondike
```
[ stock ] [ waste ]   [ F♠ ] [ F♥ ] [ F♦ ] [ F♣ ]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[ col ][ col ][ col ][ col ][ col ][ col ][ col ]
```
- 7 columns, fanned downward with overlap

### Card sizing
- Cards scale to fill available column width — no fixed point size
- Minimum readable card height: ~44pt (respects HIG tap target floor)
- On small devices (iPhone SE), verify 8-column FreeCell layout doesn't make cards unreadably narrow — may need horizontal scroll or a compressed fan overlap

## Drag and drop

- Drag a card (or legal sequence) from one column to another
- Drag target highlights when a legal drop is available — use `theme.colors.accent` at low opacity as a highlight
- Snap-back animation on illegal drop
- Tap-to-move as an alternative: tap a card, then tap the destination (good for accessibility and one-handed play)
- Auto-move to foundation: double-tap or long-press a card to send it to its foundation if a legal move exists

## Video Mode

Both games must support Video Mode (all six PiP positions) per the milestone-wide requirement established in v1.2. Card layouts reflow the same way as other games — board gets `layoutPriority(1)`, control chrome compresses per DESIGN.md §7.

Large PiP zones will compress the top control row. Free cells and foundations may need to reflow to a single row in large-zone Video Mode. Design that layout before implementation.

## Open questions before committing

1. **classicAnchorOverride hook** — needs to be added to DesignKit before this milestone starts. Confirm the DesignKit change is scoped and won't affect other games.
2. **FreeCell supermove rule** — implement from day one or simplify to single-card moves initially? Supermove is standard and expected; leaving it out will feel broken to FreeCell players. Recommend: implement it.
3. **Known-unsolvable FreeCell deals** — which deal numbers to exclude? Deals #11982 and #146692 are the two most cited unsolvable deals in the classic range. Source a complete exclusion list.
4. **Card back design** — a procedural SwiftUI pattern (dots, diamonds) or a static asset? Procedural is cleaner and themes automatically.
5. **Undo in FreeCell** — single-step or unlimited? FreeCell is a planning game; unlimited undo (step back through history) is conventional and expected. Klondike: single-step is fine.

---

## Complexity

High — this is the largest milestone since v1.2. Card design alone is a resolved design question that must be answered before a line of code is written. Drag-and-drop on a card game is more complex than any existing game's input model. The `classicAnchorOverride` DesignKit change is a prerequisite.

Rough estimate: 8–12 implementation sessions. FreeCell is the priority; Klondike can follow once the card component library is in place.
