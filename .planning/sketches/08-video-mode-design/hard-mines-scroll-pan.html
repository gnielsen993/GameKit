Variant 2: Scroll/pan in Video Mode

The Hard 16x30 board keeps its current cell size. The board becomes wider than the viewport; the user pans horizontally (and possibly vertically) with a single-finger drag to see different regions of the board.

A11Y-05 / 06.1-03 deconfliction (HIGH RISK): the existing MagnifyGesture from Plan 06.1-03 uses two-finger pinch. A new single-finger drag gesture for scroll/pan composes with it via .simultaneousGesture(...) — pinch = two-finger, pan = single-finger. BUT cell-level LongPressGesture(0.25).exclusively(before: TapGesture()) is also single-finger. The composition must distinguish "drag" from "tap" by movement threshold; misclassification = a flag-vs-pan misfire and a regression of ROADMAP P3 SC1's 50-tap zero-misfire requirement. Phase 11 research-flag fires per ROADMAP §v1.2 Research Flags.

Behavior

  • Cell size: unchanged — current 18pt floor from 06.1-03 preserved. Touch targets identical to non-Video-Mode Hard play.
  • Viewport: shows ~10 of 16 columns at a time on Pro Max with PiP-large top. User pans to reach edges.
  • Pan gesture: single-finger drag past a movement threshold (e.g. 8pt) cancels the cell tap and pans the viewport. Below threshold = cell tap fires.
  • Scroll indicators: auto-hiding edge gradient + a hairline progress bar so the user knows offscreen content exists.
  • Pinch still works: existing A11Y-05 unchanged. Pinching now feels like a "zoom into a panned region" interaction.

Pros

  • Cell size preserved — touch targets identical to v1.0 Hard play.
  • Mines/flag icons render at the same size users already know.
  • No legibility regression on any preset (CLAUDE.md §8.12 stays trivial).

Cons

  • Highest deconfliction risk. Drag-vs-tap movement threshold is a regression vector for the cell-level .exclusively(before:) composition Phase 6.1-03 preserved verbatim.
  • Adds a new gesture surface — gesture-composition research needed (Phase 11 research-flag triggers).
  • The user can lose track of where they are on a 16x30 board mid-game.
  • Animated overshoot/decay of the pan may interfere with the cell-reveal cascade animation from Phase 5.
